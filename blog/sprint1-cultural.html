Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

I’m usually a pretty laid back sort of guy and try not to sweat the small stuff. I tend to analyse situations alot and often find myself over analysing. I enjoy a challenge often jumping straight into the deep end when I see potential but occasionally overlook the steps in between.

Strengths
  • I’m, pretty creative and enjoy making projects look appealing
  • I’m open-minded and flexible with a sort of live and let die attitude
  • I dislike unresolved issues with a project and tend to poke and prod until its fixed
  • Good sense of humour
  • Enjoy working in a productive team environment

Limitations
  • I often get tunnel vision during certain activities and forget about the “bigger picture”
  • Really not a fan of public speaking
  • My english skills are not great

Which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles?

I believe my problem solving skills and creativity should help out a bunch here given the environment we’ll be working in. My english and public speaking issues could be an obstacle but will hopefully get better with practice.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

In the early stages of a project during my degree, it quickly became apparent that there was a skill gap between the group members. Initially we didn’t address this and just did our own parts of the project but when we tried to merge our work we realised that it wasn't going to work unless we were all on the same page. So we made sure that every team member had a solid understanding of what each other were doing, what tools and processes were in use and helped each other out if we were unsure.
